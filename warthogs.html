WARTHOGS

The warthog (Phacochoerus africanus) is a wild pig species native to sub-Saharan Africa. It is known for its large tusks and wart-like growths on its face.

Warthogs have a tough, hairy body with large tusks that protrude from the upper and lower jaws. Their skin is sparse, and they are relatively small compared to other wild pigs.

Warthogs are omnivores, feeding on grass, roots, fruits, and insects. They are social animals, living in groups called sounders. Warthogs use their tusks for defense and fighting.

Warthogs live in savannahs, woodlands, and grasslands across sub-Saharan Africa.
