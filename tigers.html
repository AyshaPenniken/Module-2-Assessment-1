TIGERS

The tiger (Panthera tigris) is the largest species of the big cat family, found primarily in Asia. There are several subspecies, including the Siberian tiger and the Bengal tiger.

Tigers have a muscular build, orange fur with black stripes, and sharp claws and teeth. Adult males can weigh up to 300 kg. Their powerful physique is adapted for hunting and ambushing prey.

Tigers are carnivores and solitary hunters. They use their camouflage to stalk and ambush prey, which includes large herbivores like deer and wild boar.

Tigers are found in forests, grasslands, and mangroves across Asia. They primarily hunt large mammals and can also live near water sources, such as rivers and lakes
