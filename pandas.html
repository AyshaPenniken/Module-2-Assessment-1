PANDAS

The giant panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca) is a bear species native to China. It is well known for its distinctive black-and-white coat and is a symbol of wildlife conservation.

Pandas are large, with adults weighing between 70-100 kg. They have black patches around their eyes, ears, and on their limbs. Despite their classification as carnivores, they primarily feed on bamboo.

Pandas are herbivores, mainly consuming bamboo. They are solitary animals, with a territory that they mark to avoid other pandas. Despite their size and strength, they are relatively slow-moving.

Pandas live in temperate forests with dense bamboo growth. Bamboo makes up over 99% of their diet, though they occasionally eat other plants or small animals.
